# How to Contribute

---

Derivative Intelligence is not a closed system.

It is a **community-driven, open-source initiative**.

If you believe intelligence systems should be:
- transparent  
- principled  
- explainable  
- globally accessible  

You are invited to contribute.

---

## Who Can Contribute

We welcome contributions from:

- engineers  
- researchers  
- designers  
- domain experts  
- writers  
- thinkers  

No prior affiliation is required.

---

## Ways to Contribute

---

### 1. Improve the Framework

Help refine the core documents:

- Manifesto  
- Principles  
- Terminology  
- Governance  

You can:
- suggest edits  
- clarify definitions  
- propose improvements  

---

### 2. Submit Proposals

If you have an idea for:

- new principles  
- governance changes  
- system architecture  
- alignment methods  

You can open a proposal.

---

### 3. Contribute Research

Help expand the intellectual foundation:

- comparisons (DI vs AI)  
- alignment models  
- governance frameworks  
- system design approaches  

---

### 4. Build Implementations

Start building systems aligned with DI principles:

- LLM architectures  
- alignment layers  
- explainability tools  
- governance mechanisms  

---

### 5. Raise Questions

Not everything is defined.

Open questions are valuable.

You can:
- challenge assumptions  
- highlight gaps  
- suggest alternative perspectives  

---

## How to Get Started

---

### Step 1: Explore

- Read the [Manifesto](../docs/manifesto.md)  
- Review the [Principles](../docs/principles.md)  
- Understand the [Terminology](../docs/terminology.md)  

---

### Step 2: Open an Issue

Use GitHub Issues to:

- ask questions  
- propose ideas  
- start discussions  

---

### Step 3: Submit a Pull Request

For direct contributions:

- fork the repository  
- make your changes  
- submit a pull request  

All contributions are reviewed transparently.

---

## Contribution Guidelines

---

### Be Clear

- explain your reasoning  
- provide context  
- reference existing principles when applicable  

---

### Be Constructive

- focus on improving the framework  
- avoid unnecessary complexity  

---

### Be Respectful

- encourage open dialogue  
- support diverse perspectives  

---

### Stay Aligned

All contributions should align with:

- the foundational corpus of guiding principles  
- transparency and explainability  
- long-term system integrity  

---

## What Happens Next

Contributions go through:

1. **Review**  
2. **Discussion**  
3. **Refinement**  
4. **Decision (approve / revise / reject)**  

All steps are transparent.

---

## Governance Alignment

Significant changes may:

- require formal proposals  
- go through governance processes  
- be recorded and versioned  

See: `/governance/governance-model.md`

---

## Philosophy of Contribution

This is not about ownership.

It is about building a shared foundation.

A system that:

- outlives individuals  
- remains globally accessible  
- evolves through collective effort  

---

## Final Note

We are at the beginning.

If you’re here early, you are helping define the foundation.
