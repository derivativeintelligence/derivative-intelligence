# Manifesto: Derivative Intelligence

---

We’ve been calling it Artificial Intelligence.

That framing is wrong.

There is nothing artificial about these systems.  
And they are not intelligent in the human sense.

What we are building is **Derivative Intelligence**.

---

## The Misunderstanding

Modern systems are trained on human-generated data.

They learn from:
- our language  
- our decisions  
- our knowledge  
- our patterns  

They do not originate.

They derive.

---

## The Illusion

When a system produces something new, we assume creation.

But what appears as novelty is often:
- recombination  
- optimization  
- pattern synthesis  

At scale.

Powerful—but not originative.

---

## The Distinction

Human intelligence is fundamentally different.

It creates:
- meaning  
- intent  
- direction  

It is:
- intuitive  
- emotional  
- non-linear  

It does not simply process what exists.

It generates what does not.

---

## The Error

By calling these systems “intelligent” in the human sense, we create confusion.

We:
- overestimate their capabilities  
- misunderstand their limitations  
- misplace trust  

And we begin to design systems without clear foundations.

---

## The Consequence

Today’s systems are:
- opaque  
- centrally controlled  
- difficult to audit  
- inconsistent in alignment  

We are building powerful systems without a shared framework.

---

## The Shift

We need a new foundation.

Not artificial intelligence.

**Derivative Intelligence.**

---

## The Principle

Derivative Intelligence must be grounded in a **transparent, stable corpus of guiding principles**.

This is not optional.

It is the foundation.

> If data is memory,  
> the corpus of guiding principles is the constitution.

---

## The Direction

The next generation of intelligence systems should be:

- transparent, not black box  
- grounded in guiding principles, not arbitrary policies  
- community-driven, not centrally controlled  
- open, not opaque  
- globally accessible, not concentrated  

---

## The Responsibility

These systems will shape:
- how decisions are made  
- how knowledge is accessed  
- how societies operate  

They must be built with clarity.

With accountability.

With intent.

---

## The Role of Humans

Machines will:
- process  
- optimize  
- scale  

But they will not originate.

Human intelligence remains the source of:
- meaning  
- purpose  
- direction  

---

## The Beginning

This is not a product.

It is a foundation.

It is an invitation:
- to builders  
- to researchers  
- to thinkers  

To define how intelligence systems should be built.

Together.

---

## Final Statement

**Machines derive.  
Humans originate.**
